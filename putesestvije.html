A babbling river beside you, ice cold snow melt free after a winter frozen in captivity, rushing down the mountain as if there will be no tomorrow. That’ll be you later. First, I compete against myself and against the hill, not others. Every passing year is a vain (in both senses of the word) fight against time, decay and deterioration. Age is not kind to the hill climber. VO2max declines by 1% every year, our maximum heart rate slows, our muscles weaken and recovery takes longer.
